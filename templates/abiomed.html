Data Science Coop - Abiomed inc(Johnson & Johnson)

During my role as a Data Science Co-op at Abiomed, I had the opportunity to make significant contributions to cutting-edge projects, particularly focusing on their pioneering flagship product, the Impella pump. This revolutionary device stands at the forefront of biomedical innovation, and my involvement further deepened my appreciation for the intersection of data science and groundbreaking medical technologies.

I developed machine learning models aimed at predicting cardiac output in patients undergoing mechanical circulatory support. Utilizing high-frequency time-series data from the Impella pump, obtained through a combination of pre-clinical and clinical experiments, I successfully processed the data to eliminate noise and applied windowing techniques. The outcomes demonstrated the superior predictive capabilities of my model, achieving a notable 5.2% reduction in percentage error compared to previous approaches.

I had the opportunity to explore, conduct research, and implement a Domain-Adversarial Neural Network (DANN) for predicting aortic pressure in various patient cohorts. This involved utilizing computer simulations to generate extensive datasets. Domain Adversarial Networks (DANs) represent a category of machine learning models specifically crafted to tackle challenges related to domain adaptation. These networks aim to create data representations that remain consistent across diverse source domains, allowing for more robust generalization to unfamiliar domains. The incorporation of adversarial training, where the model learns to differentiate between source and target domains, plays a crucial role in mitigating domain shift issues and enhancing the transferability of acquired features across varied datasets.

I also had the opportunity to analyze and fine-tune the predicted probability values generated by a model which predicted right heart failures. I enhanced the model's Brier skill score to be within a targeted acceptable range.
