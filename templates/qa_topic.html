Question Answering System

Aim: Develop a robust and intelligent system capable of comprehending natural language questions, and providing accurate and contextually appropriate answers.

Baseline: The baseline model utilized for the experiment involved employing a Bi-directional LSTM (Long Short Term Memory) with self-attention, complemented by bag-of-words word embedding.

BERT Model :- DistilledBERT, a quantized and pruned iteration of BERT, offers notable advantages over BERT-Base, including a 40% reduction in parameters, a 60% increase in training speed, approximately 4.5 times faster inference for standard NLP tasks, and a smaller size facilitating easier deployment and tuning, while still maintaining 95% of performance.

  • Achieved a 63.5% accuracy and 66.7 F1-score with the baseline Bi-Directional LSTM model on SQuAD2.0.
  • Achieved 77.3% accuracy with Distilled BERT, achieving an 85.4 F1-score for question-answering tasks.
